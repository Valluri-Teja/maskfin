PAN (Permanent Account Number)
A PAN is a unique 10-character alphanumeric identifier issued by the
Income Tax Department of India, used to track financial transactions
and tax liability for an individual or entity. Under India's Digital
Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act, 2023), a PAN counts as personal
data because it can identify a specific individual. Organizations
processing PAN numbers are expected to collect and store them only for
a clear, stated purpose, and to apply reasonable security safeguards
against unauthorized access or leakage.

Aadhaar Number
Aadhaar is a 12-digit unique identity number issued by the UIDAI
(Unique Identification Authority of India) to residents of India,
linked to biometric and demographic data. Aadhaar numbers are treated
as sensitive personal data. The Aadhaar Act, 2016 restricts how
Aadhaar numbers can be collected, stored, and shared, and separately,
the DPDP Act's general personal-data-protection obligations also apply
to any organization holding Aadhaar numbers, given how uniquely
identifying they are.

Bank Account Number
A bank account number, especially combined with other identifying
information like a name or IFSC code, is considered financial personal
data. Financial data of this kind is a common target in fraud and
identity theft, and the DPDP Act's core principle of data minimization
means organizations should avoid retaining or exposing account numbers
beyond what a specific transaction or service requires.

IFSC Code
An IFSC (Indian Financial System Code) identifies a specific bank
branch and is used for routing electronic fund transfers (NEFT, RTGS,
IMPS). On its own an IFSC code does not identify a person, but when
displayed alongside an account number and account holder name, it
becomes part of a set of financial identifiers that, combined, can
facilitate fraud - which is why it is treated as sensitive in this
context and redacted alongside the account number.

General principle: data minimization
A recurring theme across India's data protection framework is data
minimization - only collecting, storing, or displaying personal data
that is actually necessary for the task at hand. A redaction tool that
removes identifiers not needed for a given purpose (e.g. reviewing a
statement's transaction history without needing to see the account
holder's PAN) is a practical application of this principle.
